Gate Assessment

Gate 1 — Safety & Regulatory Compliance (Vertiport)
Obstacle surfaces clear, no Part 77 penetrations. Existing ARFF infrastructure adequate for eVTOL lithium-ion events at Vertiport scale. Minor ramp reconfiguration at B SOUTH needed (~$150–300K), achievable within existing footprint.

Gate 1 — Safety & Regulatory Compliance (Vertihub)
8-pad Vertihub exceeds available ramp geometry at 42J and requires dedicated ARFF per FAA AC 150/5210-6D — not warranted at this volume. All Vertihub scenarios score zero. This protects 42J from a $10M+ overbuild.

Unique Strategic Advantage

42J's 2,953 military helicopter operations — including H60 Black Hawks and H47 Chinooks — show the airport already manages VTOL-class aircraft harder to integrate than eVTOL. Adjacent Camp Blanding JTC creates a rare civil-military dual-use opportunity that could qualify for DoD co-investment under OTA mechanisms.

COMPETITIVE DIFFERENTIATOR
⚠️

Overbuild Protection

Every Vertihub scenario scores zero — failing Gate 1 on ARFF requirements and ramp geometry. This is the correct result for a rural GA airport. Our assessment protects Keystone Heights from over-capitalizing on infrastructure it cannot safely support at current volume. A Vertiport is the right scale.
